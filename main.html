Origin and Literal Meaning The term "lorem" comes from a truncated form of "dolorem ipsum", which translates roughly to "the pain itself" in Latin. It originates from a 1st-century BC text by the Roman philosopher Cicero, De Finibus Bonorum et Malorum, a treatise on ethics.
